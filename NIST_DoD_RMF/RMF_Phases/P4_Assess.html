NIST SP 800-53A Controls and Validations

    AC & IA Controls
  • Access Control & Identification and Authentication Controls

    • AC & IA ensure the information system is using proper key management techniques, tokens, and certificates, correct and verified authentication methods, access control mechanisms are in plance and active, passwords are controlled, etc.

    CM, SA, & SI Controls
  • Configuration Management
  • System Acquisition
  • System Integration

    • Define the completeness of understanding and development of the information system components. Controls related to this area are ones that ensure that the IS's policies and processes fully support the goal of uninterrupted operations, incorporates best practices, includes baseline inventory of all hardware and software components, is under the control of a configuration control board and has all patches, upgrades and new applications, etc.

    AU & SC Controls
  • Auditing Accountability
  • System Communication
  • Ensure the information system is:
    • Being audited and monitored to identify if security incidents are identified in a timely manner
    • Secured so that access is based on the need-to-know and least privilege
    • Implementing an effective network device control program
    • Appropriately managing privileged accounts
    • Identifying and correcting unauthorized changes to data, software, and hardware
    • Using virus protection
    • Blocking Unauthorized VoIP
    • etc.
    Network Controls
  • Ensure the information system is compliant with NIST and agency connection rules and approval processes; that VPN traffic is visible to network Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS); and remote access is controlled; etc.
    PE Controls
  • Physical and Environmental Controls
    • Ensure the IS is protected against threats related to temperature, fire, humidity, and physical intrusions.
    • These controls include actions like deploying power cutoff switches, humidity and fire detectors, guards, locks, safes, alarms, media encryption and sanitizing and destroying data media solutions.
    PS & AT Controls
  • Personnel Security and Awareness & Training
    • Ensure that the IS has acceptable user policies, training and awareness programs, and conducts background checks, clearances checked, if needed, etc.
    CP Controls
  • Contingency Planning
      • Continuity/contingency controls
    • Detail the recovery of operations due to the loss of data, components and infrastructure.
    • These controls include items such as alternate sites, continuous power supply, data backups, disaster/recovery (BDR) plans and exercises, and trusted recovery, etc.

Each control type has defined objectives, control statements, and subsequent objectives for the assessment.
