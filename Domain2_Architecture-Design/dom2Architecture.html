Data Storage and Protection

{{Data Sovereignty}}:
Regulatory compliance applies to both active and backup data. Know the laws for your storage site.

    Data Protection
  • Data Availability
    • Make sure data is protected and available under all circumstances
    • Ensures users have the data they need to contduct business
    • Even if data is damaged or lost
  • Data Management
    • Data lifecycle management is the automated process of moving critical data to online and offline storage
    • Information lifecycle management is a comprehensive strategy for valuing, cataloging, and protecting information assets from application and user errors, attacks, or hardware and facility failures.
  • Data Privacy

    • Defines who has access to the data

Recovery

  • DR - Disaster Recovery - bringing systems back online after a disaster including backup restoration
  • BCP - Business Continutity Program - get the business operating once DR is complete

Virtualization

Containters vs Virtualization

  • Containers (like Docker) virtualize the OS
  • Virtualization emulates the hardware

As A Service

  • IaaS
    • Subscription model whereby the facility is provided and you bring your own hardware
  • PaaS
    • Subscription which allows you to spin many kinds of computing/storage/networking instances (like cloud servers)
  • SaaS
    • Subscription to an application or family of applications
    • No subscriber control over the platform hosting the apps

Backup Strategy

The CompTIA answer is the Full backup + Differential is the fastest because you're only restoring the Full as a baseline and 1 change. Incremental backup represents 1:n.
